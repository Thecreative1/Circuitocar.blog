# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

**Primary user**: Someone researching a used car purchase before committing. They are comparing options, cross-referencing prices, and looking for honest guidance — not a sales pitch. They distrust generic classifieds and anonymous content. They want to feel like they found a knowledgeable person, not a marketing funnel. Secondary users include local buyers in the Famalicão, Braga, Guimarães, and Vale do Ave area who are closer to a decision and need a final confidence boost before contacting the dealership.

## Product Purpose

circuitocar.blog is the editorial arm of Circuito Car (circuitocar.pt), a second-hand car and motorcycle dealership based in Joane, Vila Nova de Famalicão. The blog publishes practical guides on used car buying, credit simulators, trade-in (retoma) tools, and local SEO articles. Its job is to earn trust before the sale: a reader who feels genuinely helped by an article is far more likely to call or WhatsApp than one who was pushed. Success looks like a reader who finishes an article thinking "these people know their stuff" and immediately contacts the team.

## Brand Personality

Sharp · Confident · Warm

Voice: the neighbourhood expert you'd trust with your own money. Direct without being blunt. Warm without being soft. The kind of person who tells you what the salesman won't. No jargon, no hand-waving, no padding.

Tone: Portuguese, specific, grounded. Famalicão is named on purpose; local specificity is a signal of accountability, not a limitation.

## Anti-references

- **Generic car classifieds** (OLX, StandVirtual): thumbnail grids, no personality, purely transactional. The blog should feel like the opposite — human, considered, expert.
- **Corporate dealership sites** (large franchise groups, BMW PT): polished but cold, feels like a machine. Circuito Car is a real family-run stand, not a brand unit.
- **Clickbait auto blogs**: stock photos, SEO-stuffed listicles, no genuine expertise. Every article must earn its place.
- **Over-designed agency work**: heavy animations, gradient-heavy hero sections, style as a substitute for substance. Design should serve the content, not compete with it.

## Design Principles

1. **Earned authority** — Signal competence through precision, not decoration. A clean typographic hierarchy and well-structured content say more than any hero graphic. Every visual choice should feel deliberate, not demonstrative.

2. **Warmth through specificity** — Generic warmth is indistinguishable from marketing. Real warmth comes from naming the town, the person answering the phone, the actual stock. Design should surface these specifics, not bury them in abstraction.

3. **Contact is the product** — The WhatsApp button and phone number are the most important elements on the page. CTAs should never feel like afterthoughts appended to content; they belong in the flow, at the moment of conviction.

4. **Content carries the weight** — Typography, spacing, and hierarchy do the design work. Graphic decoration is optional. An article that reads well, scans clearly, and flows logically is the target — not one that looks good in a screenshot.

5. **Local before national** — Vale do Ave specificity is a feature. The design should feel rooted: a real place, real people, real cars. Not a template that could belong to any dealership in any country.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum. Keyboard navigation throughout. Sufficient contrast on all text over dark and warm backgrounds. Reduce-motion preference respected for all animations. Focus indicators visible on all interactive elements.
